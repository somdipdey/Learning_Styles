How to complete this questionnaire

  • There is no time limit (it typically takes around 10–15 minutes).
  • Be as honest as you can — there are no right or wrong answers.
  • For each statement, tick it if you agree more than you disagree.
  • If you disagree more than you agree, leave it unticked.
  • Your scores update automatically. When you’re done, scroll to the Results section.

What do these styles mean?

These are short summaries of the learning styles described by Honey & Mumford.

Activist – learns by doing

Activists involve themselves fully in new experiences. They enjoy the “here and now”, like variety, and often prefer to try things first and consider consequences later. They tend to thrive on new challenges but may get bored with longer implementation and consolidation.

Reflector – learns by observing and thinking

Reflectors prefer to stand back, observe, and think things through from multiple angles. They like collecting information, listening to others, and taking time before reaching conclusions. They may dislike being rushed or pushed into the spotlight.

Theorist – learns by understanding models and logic

Theorists integrate observations into coherent, logical frameworks. They like structure, concepts, models, and clear reasoning. They prefer objective analysis and may feel uncomfortable with ambiguity, subjective topics, or approaches that don’t “make sense”.

Pragmatist – learns by applying ideas in practice

Pragmatists are keen to try out ideas and techniques to see if they work in real situations. They prefer practical tips, action plans, and immediate relevance. They may become impatient with long, open-ended discussion if it doesn’t lead to action.
